Personal Statement

Here Is Some Insight Into How I Got To Where I Am

I have always been a curious and creative person. I remember as a child I was once playing with my favorite toy, a remote-controlled car. I was filled with wonderment as I watched the car react seamlessly with the movements I made on the controller. I needed to know how this magical contraption was operating. After what seemed like an eternity of begging, I was able to convince my parents to purchase me a kit to build my own remote control car. I worked tirelessly to put the car together, and tried to make sense of how all the parts functioned. After weeks of hard work, where I allowed my curiosity to lead me in troubleshooting the car's issues, I had successfully finished. I was overcome with feelings of pride, accomplishment, and happiness.

Ever since then, I have allowed my curiosity to lead me to success in other endeavors. I attended Syracuse University where I graduated with a degree in Sport Management. While I knew I was passionate about working in the sports industry, I wasnâ€™t sure what the right niche in the industry was for me. I followed my curious nature and explored the areas of operations, sales, public relations, and marketing. I was fascinated learning the new responsibilities of each different position. I ultimately realized that while I enjoyed these different areas, they lacked the creative aspect that I was looking for.

I began to research other opportunities that I had not yet experienced. After an extensive search, I came across the growing industry of computer engineering. I learned that a degree in this field would not only open many employment opportunities to me, but it would also satiate my curious nature and creative desires. I decided to go back to school to pursue this degree with the hopes of one day combining this knowledge with the knowledge I have gained from my previous degree and work experiences.

Now that I have completed my Computer Engineering degree, I am hungry to start gaining real world experience. I believe that front end web development is a great field for me. While I am just getting started in this area, I believe my past work experiences demonstrate my ability to learn quickly and be a successful asset to a company.
